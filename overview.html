Abstract

Raptor iGEM is trying to complete the transformation of the terpenoid bornyl diphosphate synthase found in Salvia officinalis into a biobrick for quicker production and a proof of concept. The sage plant Slavia officinalis produces terpenoids that have several medicinal applications including recently-reported antifungal activity. But extraction and purification of these terpenoids from sage on the commercial scale is difficult and expensive. We propose to improve production by transferring the terpenoid synthesis pathway into Escherichia coli and yeast. An important enzyme in the terpenoid pathway is bornyl diphosphate synthetase, so we designed primers for amplifying its gene by PCR, cloning in E. coli then protein over-expression in yeast. Unfortunately, our PCR reactions did not produce prominent, gene-sized, gel bands, perhaps due to long intron sequences. We thus plan to use the same primers for cloning the mRNA as a BioBrick by reverse transcription into cDNA, thereby enabling protein over-expression in either E. coli or yeast.

Vocabulary

Salvia officinalis is a perennial evergreen shrub. It is often referred to as "common sage" or "garden sage". It has extensive history of usage in medicine.

bornyl diphosphate synthase is a catalyst enzyme terpenoid found in Salvia officinalis. By transforming this terpenoid into a biobrick, we can realize and utilize potential.

BioBricks are standardized DNA sequences that conform to an assembly standard. Serving as the base of synthetic biology, they are used to design and develop biological circuits to help construct new systems.

Terpenoids are organic compounds that plants produce naturally. Each individual plant can produce multiple terpenoids, which are designed to aid the plant in various ways. For example, terpenoids can deter herbivores, attract predators of the herbivores, protect against fungi or bacteria, attract pollinators, or act as photosynthetic pigments. Terpenoids can also act as anti-viral, anti-allergenic, or anti- bacterial drugs.
