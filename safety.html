  2. Do any of the new BioBrick parts (or devices) that you made this year raise safety issues?

    3. Unfortunately due to a lack of time and resources we were not able to submit any biological parts, therefore there was no safety issue. Although if we had submitted a working system there still would not have been a large safety issue.


  3. Is there a local biosafety group, committee, or review board at your institution?

    4. Our school does not have an overseeing committee on biosafety; however we followed the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention's protocols for dealing with BSL1.


  4. Do you have any other ideas how to deal with safety issues that could be useful for future iGEM competitions? How could parts, devices and systems be made even safer through biosafety engineering?

    5. We, at Raptor iGEM, believe that for future competitions safety should be the number one priority and awareness for safety should be increased. In order to prepare for and stop a threat this awareness is key.
