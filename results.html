Results

The 1801 nucleotide coding sequence for bornyl diphosphate synthase is known. We designed primers for amplifying its gene by PCR, cloning in E. coli then protein over-expression in yeast. Unfortunately, our PCR reactions did not produce prominent, gene-sized, or gel bands.

Conclusion

Unfortunately our team was not able to successfully insert the DNA sequence for bornyl diphosphate synthase into a plasmid in E. coli. There are likely numerous reasons for the unsuccessful insertion of the gene first and foremost money and time. But beyond those reasons there are several potential reasons why our project did not work. The first and foremost problem was how long the gene was. At nearly 2kb it is an enormous piece of DNA and hard to PCR. The next possible problem would be the primers or perhaps long intron sequences. Thus, at a later time, we plan to use the same primers for cloning the mRNA as a BioBrick by reverse transcription into cDNA, thereby enabling protein over-expression in either E. coli or yeast.
